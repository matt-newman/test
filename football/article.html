RED-FACED DEVILS

Glum Man Utd arrive back after Barcelona humiliation as Ole’s wheels fall off with five defeats in seven amid fight for Champions League spot

Crestfallen Solskjaer and his players arrived at Manchester airport in early hours of Wednesday morning after Nou Camp hammering

Pictured
By Toby Gannon
17th April 2019, 8:04 am
Updated: 17th April 2019, 8:34 am

DEFLATED Manchester United stars arrive back home after their Champions League humiliation in Barcelona.

Ole Gunnar Solskjaer and his crestfallen players were pictured walking out of Manchester airport in the early hours of this morning.

The Norwegian boss looked glum after having his European adventure cut short - but still signed autographs for waiting fans.

Lionel Messi scored twice and Philippe Coutinho curled in the third to knock United out 4-0 on aggregate.

Both David De Gea and Ashley Young dropped clangers to add to their own downfall.

United now face a battle to finish in the top four to compete in next season's Champions League.

The Red Devils are two points behind fourth-placed Arsenal in sixth with five games of the season to go starting with Everton on Sunday.

Solskjaer admitted he has a huge rebuilding job on his hands after suffering five defeats in his last seven.

But optimistic Jesse Lingard is already eyeing Champions League redemption next season.

He told BT Sport: "The Champions League is a big competition and we'll be fighting to get back in. We’ve got a big week ahead."

Lingard, 26, added: "I thought we dominated in the first 15 minutes.

"We had a couple of chances and the game could have gone differently if we'd put them away.

"That's football. Chances in these big games are massive and if we take them it's a different game.

"We were always going to be underdogs coming into the game, but we had to back ourselves and believe and, like I said, for the first 15 minutes if it had gone our way it would have been a different game."
